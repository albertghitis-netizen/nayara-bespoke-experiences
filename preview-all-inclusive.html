The Short Answer

Is Nayara Gardens All-Inclusive?

In short: No, Nayara Gardens is not all-inclusive. Your stay includes a full daily breakfast plus the rainforest setting and the resort grounds. Other meals and guided experiences are added as you choose. That flexibility suits Costa Rica, a country made to be explored rather than fenced in.

No is the honest answer, and it is worth understanding what that actually means for your trip.

Nayara Gardens builds a full breakfast into every nightly rate, then leaves the rest of the day open. You are not buying a wristband and a fixed set of buffets. You are booking a rainforest resort at the foot of the Arenal Volcano and choosing how to spend your time, and your budget, from there.

For most families that is a feature, not a gap. The best of Arenal, the waterfalls, the wildlife, the hot springs, and the family-run restaurants of La Fortuna, sits just outside the gate. This guide lays out exactly what is included, what is not, and how to plan a stay that is not all-inclusive.

What's Included

What Your Stay Includes

Here is what comes with a stay at Nayara Gardens, before you add a single thing:

  • A full daily breakfast, included for every guest, every morning.
  • The rainforest setting and resort grounds, including the gardens, trails, and the wildlife that lives on the property.
  • The swimming pools and the everyday comforts of the resort.
  • Wi-Fi and the service of the team, from arrival to check-out.

What is added as you go: lunch and dinner across the restaurants, spa treatments, and guided experiences such as wildlife walks, hanging bridges, and ziplines. You pay for what you choose, when you choose it.

A Quick Definition

What “All-Inclusive” Usually Means

An all-inclusive rate bundles your room, meals, drinks, and often some activities into one price paid up front. You check in, and most of what you consume on property is already covered. It is a model built for beach resorts where guests rarely leave, and it can genuinely simplify a trip when the resort is the destination.

Costa Rica is a different kind of trip. A handful of all-inclusive resorts operate here, mostly on the Guanacaste beach coast, but the country as a whole is not an all-inclusive destination. Most of its best stays, Nayara Gardens among them, include breakfast and let you choose the rest.

Why It Works Here

Why Pay-As-You-Go Suits a Costa Rica Trip

The pleasure of Arenal is that it is not contained. Some days you will want breakfast on property and dinner in La Fortuna. Some days you will be out on a hanging-bridge walk through lunch. An all-inclusive rate asks you to pay for meals whether or not you are there to eat them.

Choosing as you go means your budget follows your days. You can eat well at the resort, try the town, and put the savings toward the experiences that make an Arenal trip, the wildlife guide, the waterfall, the volcano itself.

It is the way most seasoned Costa Rica travelers go: resort comfort, without the wristband deciding your day.

A Day, Unpacked

A Sample Day of Dining and Adventure

Breakfast is included, so the morning starts without a bill: fresh fruit, strong Costa Rican coffee, and the rainforest waking up around you. From there the day is yours.

Mid-morning, a guided walk finds sloths and toucans on the property. Lunch might be light at the resort, or in La Fortuna after a waterfall stop. The afternoon holds a hot-spring soak or an easy hike, and dinner is a proper sit-down, whether you choose the resort restaurant or somewhere new.

Nothing about the day was fixed at check-in, and nothing was wasted. You paid for the breakfast you ate and the experiences you chose, and skipped the ones you did not.

Planning Ahead

How to Plan and Budget Your Stay

A non-all-inclusive stay is easy to plan once you know the shape of it:

  • Breakfast is covered. Budget for lunches and dinners, at the resort or in town.
  • Pick a few experiences in advance. Wildlife walks, hanging bridges, and hot springs are worth pre-booking in busy months.
  • Leave room to be spontaneous. Some of the best meals and stops in Arenal are the ones you find along the way.
  • Ask the resort to help. The team can arrange dining and experiences before you arrive if you would rather have the plan set.
