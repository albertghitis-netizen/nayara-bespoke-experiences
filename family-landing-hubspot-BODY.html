A Jungle That Teaches

Where a family vacation becomes the kind of education no textbook can give.

Nayara Gardens sits inside Arenal Volcano National Park, woven into living Costa Rican rainforest. For children, that means every path is a lesson and every day holds a discovery: a sloth mapped by name, a poison frog glimpsed by flashlight, a howler monkey calling from the canopy. Wonder does the teaching here, and the whole family gets to learn. Nayara Gardens is the original Nayara, and it welcomes families and couples alike.

The Neighbors Are Wild

Resident Sloths

Slow and Unforgettable

Frogs After Dark

The Night Shift

Explore Together

The Rainforest at Your Feet

The grounds themselves are living rainforest. Garden paths wind beneath the canopy, and wildlife is simply part of daily life here: sloths in the trees overhead, hummingbirds in the heliconia, the jungle waking the family each morning. No excursion required.

Guides point out what most eyes would miss: a camouflaged bird, a sleeping sloth, a column of leaf-cutter ants carrying the forest piece by piece. It is exploration with a teacher built in.

Made for Families

Wild Days, Easy Stays

Adventure is the point, but so is ease. Free-standing villas give families room to spread out, with daybeds for the kids and a private garden of their own. Rooms come stocked with fresh fruit and snacks, and golf carts ferry tired explorers wherever they need to go.

They have thought of the small things too, with bug lotion in the room and sunscreen at every pool, so parents can relax while the rainforest does the entertaining. For the braver explorers, ziplines run over the canopy just minutes from the resort.

For the Grown-Ups

Good to Know

What ages is Nayara Gardens best for?+
Every age. Free-standing villas sleep families comfortably, the pools have shallow sections for the youngest swimmers, and the wildlife and night walks delight toddlers through teenagers. The setting is romantic too, so couples and multigenerational groups feel just as at home.
Do we need to leave the resort to see wildlife?+
No. The grounds are living rainforest and a wildlife reserve, so sloths, monkeys, frogs, and tropical birds are part of daily life on the property. Guided walks, including after dark, happen right on site, with no excursion required.
What activities are included?+
Daily guided wildlife walks, garden trails, the volcano-fed hot-spring pools, and the rainforest setting come with your stay. Night walks, ziplines nearby, and other guided experiences can be arranged through the resort.
Is it easy to get around with kids?+
Yes. Paths between the villas, pools, and restaurants are short and stroller-friendly, and golf carts are on call to ferry tired explorers. Villas are stocked with fruit and snacks, and the small things, from bug lotion to poolside sunscreen, are handled.
