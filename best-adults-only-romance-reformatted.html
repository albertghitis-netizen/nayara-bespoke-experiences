The Case for Child-Free Travel

Why Adults-Only Travel Is the Smartest Romantic Choice

In short: the most romantic Nayara stays are the two adults-only resorts, Nayara Springs in Costa Rica and Nayara Bocas del Toro in Panama. Alto Atacama and Hangaroa are not adults-only, but their remoteness makes them just as romantic.

Escaping routine is not indulgence. It is a documented psychological need, and the research on how couples grow together is surprisingly unambiguous.

Self-expansion theory, developed by Arthur Aron and colleagues, holds that people grow through their closest relationships, and that couples who take on novel experiences together report more satisfaction, passion, and physical intimacy. A 2024 study in the Journal of Positive Psychology confirmed it for travel specifically: self-expanding trips predicted higher passion and intimacy whether a couple had been together three months or thirty years. What mattered was not how often they traveled, but the quality of what they shared.

The instinct is widely felt. The U.S. Travel Association found that 61% of couples say a trip reignited their romance, and a quarter discovered a side of their partner they had never seen. Three in four, by one survey, call traveling together the ultimate relationship test.

Which is where adults-only stops being a luxury and becomes a design decision. Distraction dilutes the very presence that makes travel transformative. Remove it, and the experience concentrates. The market has noticed. The Telegraph counts more than a thousand adults-only properties worldwide, searches for child-free holidays have climbed every year since 2020, and bookings rose roughly 20% in 2025. The typical guest is between 30 and 50 and willing to pay a family's rate for two. This was never about saving money. It is about protecting attention.

Nayara Springs · Costa Rica

Costa Rica's Most Romantic Adults-Only Resort

Nayara Springs was built on a single idea: an otherworldly adults-only retreat in the heart of a living rainforest. Three Michelin Keys, Relais & Châteaux, and a former number-one ranking on TripAdvisor all followed from a philosophy that treats privacy as architecture.

Every freestanding villa has its own plunge pool fed by volcanic hot-spring water, warmed by the magma beneath Arenal and rich in the calcium, magnesium, and silica that have restored people here for centuries. Tropical gardens screen each villa into its own world. No shared walls, no common corridors, no reason to see another guest unless you want to.

What makes it feel adults-only is not the policy but the pace. No posted schedule, no morning announcements, no group activities competing for your attention. Dining follows the same logic: open-air tables beneath the canopy, menus built around the rainforest, romance treated as a ritual rather than a reservation.

Nayara Bocas del Toro · Panama

An Adults-Only Private Island in the Caribbean

On a private island in Panama's Bocas del Toro archipelago, the same philosophy meets open water. Overwater villas float above clear turquoise shallows, each with a saltwater plunge pool, a wraparound deck, and a staircase descending straight into the sea. You wake to waves against the stilts and watch fish glide beneath glass floor panels. Two Michelin Keys, Forbes recognition, Green Globe certification, and the number-one resort in Central America for 2025.

Here the model is all-inclusive by design. No check to sign, no tip to calculate, no deciding whether something is worth it. A second bottle of wine is simply a yes. Midnight room service feels like a gift rather than a line item. The whole island runs at the tempo of two people.

Dinner is served in the Elephant House, a century-old structure shipped from Bali, where the menu changes nightly and stingrays drift past in the moonlit water below. Balinese-inspired villas, four-poster beds beneath Tumpang Sari canopies, hand-carved teak, and fire pits on private decks complete a place that feels at once ancient and entirely yours.

Beyond Adults-Only

Romance at Atacama and Easter Island

Two more Nayara properties earn a place in any romantic itinerary, not through an age policy but through sheer geography. Neither is adults-only. Neither needs to be. Their isolation does what no door policy ever could.

Nayara Hangaroa sits on Rapa Nui, the most remote inhabited island on Earth, some 3,700 kilometers from the Chilean mainland and 2,000 from its nearest neighbor. To arrive here together is its own kind of vow. The hotel draws its forms from the island itself: rooms curve like the ancient caves that inspired them, bathtubs are hand-shaped from clay and fired in local ovens, and floor-to-ceiling windows dissolve the line between room and open Pacific. A member of The Leading Hotels of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage site, its luxury is Polynesian rather than European, warm and unhurried, carried by Rapa Nui staff and ancestral story. Stand before the fifteen Moai of Ahu Tongariki at sunrise, carved centuries ago and weighing as much as 86 tonnes, and you will not be sharing the moment with a tour bus. You will be sharing it with one person.

Nayara Alto Atacama works in the opposite direction, building intimacy through subtraction. The Atacama is the driest non-polar desert on Earth, and in its thin, silent air sensory clutter falls away and attention sharpens. Research in the Journal of Environmental Psychology ties low-stimulus surroundings to deeper reflection, and studies of awe show that feeling small together only strengthens a bond. Set within the Catarpe Valley, a living desert oasis where rock walls absorb sound and distance filters every movement, the resort lets couples feel alone without ever feeling isolated. Then night falls and the sky becomes the event: roughly 70% of the world's astronomy is built in this desert, and the core of the Milky Way hangs directly overhead, framed against a universe of two trillion galaxies. Time stretches. Silence gains texture. Proximity gains meaning.

The Common Thread

Romance Is Not Provided. It Is Protected.

Springs and Bocas protect it through curation and exclusivity. Atacama protects it through the subtraction of the desert. Hangaroa protects it through sheer distance. Four very different places, one conviction: a great resort does not manufacture romance, it clears away everything that competes with it.

Choose the fire of Arenal or the turquoise of Panama, the silence of the Atacama or the ancient wonder of Rapa Nui, and the result is the same. Days that belong only to you, nights that linger, and the kind of memories that become the stories you tell for the rest of your lives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Nayara resorts are adults-only?+
Nayara Springs in Costa Rica and Nayara Bocas del Toro in Panama are fully adults-only. Nayara Alto Atacama and Nayara Hangaroa welcome all ages, but their remote settings make them deeply romantic in their own right.
Why is an adults-only resort better for couples?+
It removes the noise, scheduling, and competing obligations that dilute presence. Research links novel, shared, low-distraction experiences to higher relationship satisfaction, passion, and intimacy, which is the experience an adults-only resort is designed to protect.
Is Nayara Springs or Bocas del Toro better for a honeymoon?+
Springs suits couples who want rainforest seclusion, volcanic hot-spring plunge pools, and a la carte dining. Bocas suits those who want an overwater, all-inclusive private island. Many couples pair the two.
Are these resorts all-inclusive?+
Nayara Bocas del Toro is all-inclusive by design, with dining, drinks, and most experiences included. Nayara Springs is a la carte, so dining and spa treatments are arranged as you go.
What makes Nayara Springs so highly rated?+
It holds three Michelin Keys, belongs to Relais & Châteaux, and has ranked number one in the world on TripAdvisor. The recognition follows from a design that treats privacy as architecture.
