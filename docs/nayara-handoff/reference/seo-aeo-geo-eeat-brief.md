# Strategy Brief: SEO vs AEO vs GEO, and EEAT underneath it all

_Nayara Resorts. June 20, 2026. Companion to the Definitive Digital Growth Report and the Semrush
Competitive Analysis. Data points (AI sessions, reviews, awards, keyword counts) from Windsor.ai
connectors, Semrush, and competitive research. Authored by Albert Ghitis, Head of Digital Marketing._

Search is no longer one discipline. It is four overlapping ones, each rewarding something different.
This brief defines them, contrasts them, and maps each to Nayara using the live data.

## The one-minute version

SEO gets you the blue link. AEO gets you the answer box. GEO gets you named inside the AI's reply. EEAT
is the trust that decides whether any of the three pick you. They are not competing strategies; they are
layers of the same job, and Nayara is strong on the raw material for the hardest one (trust) while
under-built on the mechanics of all four.

**Why this matters for Nayara right now.** Gardens already receives 1,501 AI-assistant sessions in 90
days and converts zero, because the AI engines describe it with the wrong audience. That is not an SEO
problem; it is a GEO and EEAT problem. Meanwhile Nayara holds exactly the trust signals (3 MICHELIN Keys,
Conde Nast number one in Central America, 4.9 stars on 1,069 reviews) that all four disciplines reward,
and almost none are exposed in markup or surfaced in content. The opportunity is to convert trust Nayara
already has into visibility it does not.

## The four disciplines, defined

- **SEO (Search Engine Optimization).** Ranking in the traditional blue links. The foundation. Rewards
  relevance, backlinks and authority, technical health, content depth and freshness. KPI: rankings,
  organic clicks, keyword count.
- **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization).** Being the direct answer: featured snippets, "people also ask,"
  voice, position zero. Rewards concise answers, FAQ and HowTo and structured Q&A, schema, clear
  question-to-answer mapping. KPI: answer-box and PAA captures, voice presence.
- **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization).** Being cited or recommended inside an AI answer (ChatGPT,
  Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews). Rewards a clear consistent brand entity, presence in the
  sources LLMs draw on (Wikipedia, listicles, Reddit, reviews), freshness, quotable facts, third-party
  corroboration. KPI: recommend / mention / omit per engine.
- **EEAT (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust).** Not a technique but Google's quality
  framework, and the trust substrate the other three depend on. Rewards first-hand experience (real
  stays, real photos), demonstrable expertise (named authors), authority (awards, citations), trust
  (reviews, accuracy, transparency). KPI: review equity, award visibility, authorship.

**How they stack.** EEAT is the foundation, SEO the ground floor, AEO and GEO the two upper floors. You
cannot win the answer box or the AI citation without the relevance and trust underneath. One asset often
serves several layers: a well-structured FAQ with schema helps SEO, AEO, and GEO at once; review schema
feeds both AEO rich results and EEAT trust.

## Side by side

| | SEO | AEO | GEO | EEAT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Where it shows up | Blue-link results | Snippets, PAA, voice | Inside AI answers | Underneath all |
| The win | Rank in top results | Be the quoted answer | Be named / recommended | Be believed |
| Primary levers | Content, links, technical | FAQ + schema + concision | Entity + third-party sources | Awards, reviews, real experience |
| Time to move | Months | Weeks to months | Weeks (freshness-sensitive) | Slow, compounding |
| Nayara status | Under-built vs competitors | Schema missing | Mis-categorized | Strong assets, unexposed |

The pattern: Nayara's weakness is not the trust layer (EEAT raw material is excellent), it is the
plumbing that turns trust into visibility across the other three.

## EEAT: the trust layer underneath

| Pillar | What it means for a resort | Nayara's asset | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Experience | First-hand on-property proof: real photography, genuine detail | Real-photography mandate; genuine Journal storytelling | Have it, under-attributed |
| Expertise | Named credible authors; subject knowledge | Journal content exists | No author entities / bylines in schema |
| Authoritativeness | Awards, press, citations | 3 MICHELIN Keys (Springs), Conde Nast number one Central America (Bocas), Forbes/Tripadvisor | Not in structured data or surfaced consistently |
| Trust | Reviews, accuracy, transparency | 4.9 on 1,069 (Gardens), 523 (Springs), 363 (Tented) | AggregateRating absent from markup |

**The unlock.** Nayara's awards and reviews are exactly the signals all four disciplines reward, and they
are sitting unused. Exposing them (AggregateRating schema with real review numbers, award entities in
structured data, named author bylines) is low effort and pays into SEO rich results, AEO eligibility, GEO
citation-worthiness, and EEAT trust at the same time. This is the highest-leverage shared fix.

## One property through all four lenses (Gardens)

| Lens | Gardens' situation | The fix |
|---|---|---|
| SEO | Page-one for rainforest terms but position 20 for "kid friendly"; 408 organic keywords vs The Springs' 5,080 | Family rainforest hub page + the family/rainforest content cluster |
| AEO | Searchers ask "is nayara gardens all inclusive" and "is it good for families"; no structured answer | FAQPage schema answering family and inclusion questions directly |
| GEO | 1,501 AI sessions, zero conversions; engines narrate Gardens as adults-only/romance; kaik.ai already referring | Fix the entity (consistent family description across site, GBP, Wikipedia/Wikidata); get into the listicles and reviews LLMs cite |
| EEAT | 4.9 on 1,069 reviews and real rainforest experience, none exposed in markup or framed as family proof | AggregateRating schema; real family-stay photography; named authors |

**The insight.** Gardens being read as adults-only is primarily a GEO and EEAT problem, not a classic SEO
one. You could rank number one for "Nayara Gardens" and still have ChatGPT tell a family to book Springs
instead. Correcting the entity and exposing the trust signals fixes the layer actually suppressing family
demand.

## The engine playbook

GEO is platform-specific: only about one in ten cited sources overlaps between ChatGPT and Perplexity.

| Engine | How it chooses | Nayara move |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT | Bing index; favors Wikipedia and encyclopedic sources | Strengthen Wikipedia / Wikidata with the family fact explicit; pursue authoritative third-party coverage |
| Perplexity | Real-time retrieval; freshness-biased; leans on Reddit and reviews | Keep the Journal fresh on a cadence; encourage genuine Reddit and review discussion; date-stamp updates |
| Google AI Overviews | Gemini signals; rewards consistent cross-platform entity authority | Entity consistency across site, GBP, and schema; the EEAT markup feeds this |
| Answer boxes / voice (AEO) | Concise, schema-labeled answers | FAQ schema on every property and the "Which Nayara" page; answer the real Search Console questions verbatim |

## What to do, and how to measure it

**Shared fixes (one action, multiple layers):**
1. Hotel / LodgingBusiness + AggregateRating schema on every property.
2. FAQPage schema answering the real Search Console questions ("is it all inclusive," "good for families").
3. Award entities in structured data and on-page (MICHELIN Keys, Conde Nast).
4. Named author bylines in the Journal.
5. Entity consistency across site, Google Business Profile, Wikipedia/Wikidata (the core GEO fix,
   especially the Gardens family entity).

**The AEO/GEO scoreboard.** Traditional rank tracking does not capture AEO or GEO. Keep a fixed prompt
log, run before and after the fixes, across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews:
- "Best family resort in Arenal / Costa Rica"
- "Is Nayara Gardens good for families?"
- "Nayara Gardens vs Nayara Springs"
- "Best hot springs resort in Costa Rica" (Springs)

For each, log: does the engine recommend, mention, or omit the property, and how does it frame the
audience. That log proves the GEO work moved the needle, the way rankings prove SEO.

## Bottom line

Do not treat these as four projects. Treat EEAT exposure (schema for awards and reviews, named authors,
entity consistency) as one foundational workstream that lifts all four layers at once, then layer the SEO
content clusters and the per-engine GEO tactics on top. Nayara already has the trust; the work is making
the machines see it.
