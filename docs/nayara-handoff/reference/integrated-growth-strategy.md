Across organic search, AI answer engines, and paid media, Nayara
reads the same way: a small set of beloved properties with the
highest guest sentiment in their market, and almost no presence in
the conversations where travelers and machines decide where to
stay. The brand is found by name and rarely on the categories it
should own.
Three forces hold it back, and they are connected. First, demand is brand-led: people who already know Nayara find it, but the
property names lose to intermediaries and the category terms are barely contested. Second, the measurement underneath both
paid and organic is broken in specific, fixable ways, so the numbers leadership has seen are partly artifacts. Third, the brand's
authority and its content are scattered across a subdomain and separate sites instead of compounding on one domain.
The plan is one move with several parts: consolidate the editorial hub, the content, and the brand home onto nayararesorts.com so
authority compounds; fix the measurement gate so paid and organic can be steered; build the category and family pages the brand
is missing; and point paid media at those pages so one asset earns its keep three times over, as an ad landing page, an organic
ranking page, and the answer AI engines cite.

0 3 O R G A N I C S E A R C H
Where the demand leaks
Nayara earns substantial organic revenue, but almost entirely from people who
already know the brand. On the category terms that introduce new travelers, the
brand is on page one of demand and page two of results.
The clearest example: "costa rica resorts" draws about 10,091 searches a quarter where a Nayara page appears, and the page
sits at position 9 with a 0.55% click rate. Across the corporate site, non-brand queries make up roughly 15% of impressions but
under 4% of clicks, and about a third of even those non-brand clicks are misspellings of the brand name. True non-brand discovery is
a trickle.

TH E TH R EE R EPAI R S, I N P R I O R I TY O R DER
1. The SynXis cross-domain loop. The booking confirmation hands visitors across domains,
where analytics restarts the session and credits the booking to a "referral" from our own
site. This is why 66% of Gardens bookings are mis-credited, against 21% at Tented and 11%
at Springs. It distorts every channel's true contribution.
2. The Springs conversion tag. It broke in May, so Google Ads sees $0 of Springs revenue
and bids blind on a property that is in fact generating over half a million dollars from paid.
3. The Gardens value tracking. It reports booking values near zero against real revenue, so
its campaigns optimize on a phantom.
None of these is a strategy question; all are configuration fixes. They are listed first in the plan because everything downstream, the
honest ROAS by property, the landing-page tests, and the brand-home test, depends on them.
0 7 T H E E D I T O R I A L M I G R AT I O N
Move the Journal onto the main domain
The brand's editorial hub is good and lives in the wrong place.
blog.nayararesorts.com is a strong library of conservation, wellness, and awards
storytelling, already titled Journal, but it sits on a subdomain where its authority
does not fully count toward the domain the properties depend on.
Moved to nayararesorts.com/journal, that authority consolidates onto the root domain. Google credits a subfolder to the main
domain far more reliably than a subdomain, so every article then strengthens the same domain the property and category pages live
under, and the whole hub builds the corpus AI engines cite. The migration is the foundation that makes the content and the brand
home work harder than they could alone. The rule for the move is to redirect almost everything page by page, and prune only
genuine dead weight.
Redirect and keep (the majority)
Quality, evergreen, or commercially relevant. One-to-one 301 to /journal.
The Michelin Keys and Condé Nast Bocas award pieces; the family rainforest piece on Gardens; Best
Adults-Only Resorts for Romance; Pura Vida; the wildlife conservation series, Atacama flamingos,
Central America biodiversity, Women's Empowerment in La Fortuna, and the Nayara Horizons essays;
The Summer of You and the culinary feature.

How the pieces fit together
The migration, the new pages, the brand home, and paid media are not separate
projects. They are one structure: nayararesorts.com as a consolidated demand
engine, where every layer feeds the others.
N AYARARESORTS. C OM
The brand home, the front door
Routes visitors by intent, presents each property as a distinct entity, and pushes qualified traffic toward a
booking path.
The pillar and landing pages, the rooms
Family, the comparison, best luxury resorts, hot springs, glamping, overwater. Each serves the ad, the
organic ranking, and the AI answer.
The Journal, the authority layer
Editorial and awards that build trust and AI citations, linking up into the pillars so the link equity flows where
it converts.
Everything cross-links, so a link earned by a Journal essay strengthens the pillar page it points to, which strengthens the home,
which routes back out. Paid plugs into the middle layer: the family campaign and the non-brand category campaigns land on the
pillar pages, which ends the homepage dumping the paid audit found and makes one asset do several jobs.
The brand-home A/B test
Corporate is the widest reach in the portfolio at about 49,000 sessions a month, but at 33% engagement, 1.4 pages a visit, and
effectively no conversion. A new home should route by intent, separate the three property entities (the front-door fix for the AI
conflation), and push visitors toward booking. One caution governs the test: because bookings happen cross-domain in the booking
