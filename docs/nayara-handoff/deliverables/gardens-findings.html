Gardens findings, and how we found them

The facts we established about Nayara Gardens, and the live-data method behind them, so the team can trust the conclusions and act on them.

June 2026 · Live sources: Google Analytics, Search Console, Semrush, Instagram, Google Business Profile, the SynXis booking workbook

The findings

Measurement

  • Gardens organic earns ~$53K in 90 days vs Springs ~$343K and Tented ~$867K, on similar organic traffic. Gardens earns about one sixth of Springs on comparable visitors. GA4
  • Two Google Ads conversion-value tags are broken: Springs reports $0, Gardens $130, while GA4 shows them earning $1.4M and $0.9M. ~$41K per quarter is bid blind. Tented works (~95x). GA4 + Ads
  • The corporate site is the real blind spot: 153,839 sessions in 90 days, ~$0 tracked, because bookings happen on the property domains. GA4
  • Search Console confirms Gardens is invisible for family: it ranks #1 for "best family resorts in costa rica" but got 1 impression in 90 days. Organic clicks are almost all its own brand name plus dining. Search Console

Positioning

  • The audience is already family: 60.8% female, ages 25 to 44, US 71.6% of users and 88.4% of revenue, UK 6.6% of revenue. GA4
  • But the live page still says "Luxury Hotel in La Fortuna," AI assistants leave Gardens out of "best family resort" answers, and third-party pages re-cast it as couples. live page + AI audit
  • Gardens is cut off from its history: old names and awards are not unified under the current name; the sloth sanctuary is sometimes credited to Springs. listings + AI audit
  • Organic is declining: share fell from the high 20s/30s% (2023) to the mid teens; estimated organic traffic is down ~37% since December. KPI workbook + Semrush

Demand and keywords (US)

  • Family cluster is ~4,500 a month (family resorts 1,600, best family resorts 1,300, for families 720): modest volume but winnable white space, no rival owns family-in-Arenal. Semrush
  • Biggest winnable geo term: "hotels in la fortuna costa rica" 3,600, competition 0.20. Semrush
  • Head terms are listicle-owned (costa rica resorts 22,200); all-inclusive 49,500 is wrong intent (Nayara is breakfast-only). Semrush + SERP
  • Sloths have near-zero direct search volume but are the differentiator and the AI and social hook. Semrush

Competitors

  • Tabacon owns "hot springs"; The Springs owns generic "Arenal resort" (beatable); neither owns family in Arenal. Semrush
  • The Papagayo chains (Four Seasons, Andaz, Nekajui, Waldorf) own beach and new-opening hype but cannot answer rainforest, hot springs, sloths, or family. Semrush + AI audit

Channels

  • AI Assistant channel is real and converting: Gardens $15,350 in 90 days, fastest-growing referrer; ChatGPT is the corporate site's 3rd-largest source. GA4
  • Google Business Profile: name corrected to Nayara Gardens; 67,898 impressions, 4.9 / 1,070 reviews; "restaurants" is the #1 non-brand search (3,543), but no menus attached and Maps booking is off. Business Profile
  • Instagram: Gardens is the weakest property account (17,282) but most US-weighted (51%); Tented is the standout (64,706 followers, 90-day reach 143,773, 3 to 4x the others). Instagram
  • Average booking value: Tented $5,978 > Springs $4,129 > Gardens $3,184. booking workbook

How we found this

This was produced in Claude Code working directly against live data connectors, not a static audit or guesswork. Three things make it hold up.

1. Live data, pulled in real time through connectors

Windsor.ai

Google Analytics, Google Ads, Search Console, Instagram, and Google Business Profile, queried live for the trailing 90 days.

Semrush

Keyword volumes, live search results, competitor authority and traffic, and backlinks.

Google Drive

Your existing internal reports and the SynXis-validated booking workbook.

The result

Numbers are measured, not estimated: revenue from Analytics, rankings from Search Console, volume from Semrush.

2. SEO and marketing analysis run as structured workflows

On top of the raw data we ran dedicated passes in parallel and fed one synthesis: keyword-universe research, competitor intelligence (the Arenal independents and the Papagayo chains), an AI-visibility audit testing what assistants actually say about Gardens, local and Maps analysis, and a social benchmark.

3. Every key finding is cross-verified, and disagreements are stated

  • Gardens' revenue: the live Analytics pull matched its own Analytics report exactly and the SynXis booking workbook ranking.
  • The organic decline: the Semrush trend and the KPI workbook's history both point the same way.
  • The family gap: Semrush volume, Search Console (1 impression), and the AI audit all confirm it.
  • We flagged what we could not fully verify (channel revenue distorted by the booking handoff; crawler-blocked listicles) rather than papering over it.
Why the report holds up: it combines the team's prior reports with fresh live data from every major channel, reconciles them, and separates proven facts from tests to prove. The conclusions are not one tool's opinion; they are where Google Analytics, Search Console, Semrush, Instagram, the Maps profile, and the booking data independently agree.

For accuracy: the value came from the live connectors and the cross-verified analysis, not from any third-party SEO plugin.
