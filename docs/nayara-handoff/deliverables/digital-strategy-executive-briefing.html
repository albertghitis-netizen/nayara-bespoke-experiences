Nayara Resorts · Digital Strategy

The demand is already here. We are losing it to measurement and positioning.

A plain-language summary of where our organic, paid, social, and AI search stand across the three Costa Rica resorts and the brand, and the handful of moves that would matter most.

Executive briefing · June 2026 · Built on live Google Analytics, Google Ads, Search Console, Business Profile, Instagram, and Semrush data

The big picture

Our problem is not that people are not looking for us. They are. The issue is that we cannot always see what is working, our strongest resort is being described as the wrong kind of hotel, and most of our paid budget buys back people who were already searching for us by name.

Three things would change the trajectory: fix the measurement so we can trust our numbers, tell Nayara Gardens' family story everywhere it counts, and shift spend toward demand we do not already own.

What we are seeing, and what we suggest

Each finding below is paired with a recommendation. Some are proven fixes; others are tests we would run to confirm before committing.

Proven fix

Two revenue-tracking tags appear to be under-reporting

For two of the three resorts, the paid-search system shows almost no booking value over 90 days, while Analytics shows those same resorts earning well over a million dollars. The automated bidding is effectively running blind on this spend.

Recommendation: repair the two conversion-value tags first. Nothing else in paid can be judged fairly until the numbers are trustworthy.

Proven fix

Our most-visited site cannot connect its visitors to bookings

The corporate site drew more than 150,000 visits in 90 days, but almost none can be tied to a booking, because reservations happen on the individual resort sites. A large amount of brand, social, and paid effort currently cannot be proven to pay off.

Recommendation: close this cross-site tracking gap so we can finally see which marketing leads to which booking.

Proven fix

Nayara Gardens is being described as a couples retreat

Gardens is our family flagship, and its actual audience is mostly families and women aged 25 to 44. Yet its own page is still titled like a generic luxury hotel, and outside review sites and AI assistants describe it as a honeymoon property. When someone asks an AI for the best family resort in Costa Rica, Gardens does not appear at all.

Recommendation: tell the family story plainly on the Gardens page, in its data labels, and in the answers AI tools read, so the right guests recognize it as theirs.

Proven fix

The most common non-brand Maps search for two resorts is "restaurants"

On Google Maps, more people search "restaurants" near Gardens and Springs than almost any other non-brand term, yet our menus are not attached to the profiles and the reservation link is not turned on. This is free, high-intent demand we are not catching.

Recommendation: add restaurant menus to each Business Profile and enable a "Check availability" booking link.

Worth testing

Most paid search money defends our own name

The large majority of paid spend goes to people already typing "Nayara." The one small campaign aimed at new, non-brand demand returns roughly forty-five dollars for every dollar spent, which suggests there is room to grow beyond brand defense.

Recommendation (test): hold back brand ads in one region for a set period to measure how many of those bookings we would have won anyway, then move the savings into tracked non-brand campaigns.

Proven fix

The AI search channel is small but already booking

Visits that arrive from AI assistants are still a small share, but they convert, and they are our fastest-growing source. Gardens leads here. This is where clear, well-structured answers pay off first.

Recommendation: add the structured answer content (rooms, family, sloths, what is included) that AI tools quote, so we earn these answers rather than ceding them to competitors.

The three resorts, in one line each

Nayara Gardens
The family flagship · needs the most attention

Strong demand and a 4.9 rating from more than 1,000 reviews, but mislabeled as couples, converting below its siblings, and missing from family search answers. This is where focused work pays off most.

Nayara Springs
Adults-only romance · healthy

Correctly understood by search and AI as the romance and honeymoon choice, and the only three-MICHELIN-Key hotel in the country. Maintain and protect, and fix its revenue-tracking tag.

Nayara Tented Camp
Luxury glamping · strongest performer

Our best-converting resort and the clear leader for glamping searches. Its tracking works and its returns are excellent. Keep doing what works.

The advantage worth protecting: the large beach resorts on the Pacific coast outspend us on the broad "best Costa Rica resort" terms, but none of them can offer rainforest, a volcano, hot springs, sloths on the property, or a true family resort near Arenal. That is open ground that is ours to claim.

What we suggest doing first

  1. Repair the two revenue-tracking tags so paid spend can be judged honestly.
  2. Connect the corporate site's visits to bookings so brand and social effort becomes provable.
  3. Turn on menus and a booking link on every Google Business Profile. Quick and free.
  4. Retell the Gardens family story on its page, its labels, and the answers AI tools read.
  5. Build the family and "best luxury resorts" pages, and get Gardens added to the family roundups that AI tools quote today.
  6. Test holding back brand ads in one region, then reinvest in tracked non-brand demand.
