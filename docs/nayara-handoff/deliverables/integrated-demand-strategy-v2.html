What We Did

One integration, every claim verified against real bookings

Using the Windsor.ai integration, we connected Nayara's marketing data into a single analysis layer: the Google advertising account, the website analytics for all four properties, Google's report of every search where a Nayara page appeared, and the Google Maps business profiles for the resorts. We examined 90 days of performance in detail plus the advertising account's full history, and we verified every advertising claim against bookings actually recorded on the website rather than trusting the ad platform's self-reported numbers. Where the two disagree, the website's booking record governs throughout this report.

One honest limit on scope: Google Ads access existed for Tented Camp only. Springs and Gardens run paid advertising in accounts we could not see. Their findings and recommendations in this report rest on the data the integration does cover for them, their website analytics, their search visibility, and their Maps profiles, and every paid recommendation for those two properties is explicitly gated on getting their ad accounts shared and audited first. Also outside this dataset: bookings made directly on Booking.com and Expedia, which never touch our analytics. Counting those requires the reservation system's source-of-business report.

The Headlines

Six findings worth the price of the whole exercise

$574,800 of Gardens bookings are credited to a glitch.

The retired nayaragardens.com website is still live and silently hands visitors into the booking flow, where the analytics restarts the session and credits the booking to a "referral" from our own domain. Gardens is not 70% dependent on referrals; its measurement is broken. Its checkout tracking also undercounts (5 checkouts recorded against 28 bookings), so several numbers leadership has seen about Gardens are artifacts.

63,000+ people per quarter search our property names. 154 reach our site.

Gardens: 29,158 searches, 39 clicks. Springs: 23,839 searches, 89 clicks. Tented: 10,145 searches, 26 clicks. The rest land on Booking.com, Expedia, and TripAdvisor, who then sell us back our own guests at 15 to 25% commission.

The best campaign in the account runs on $6 a day.

The Family campaign returns $127 in website-verified bookings per dollar spent and is capped by its own tiny budget. The Wellness campaign returns 101x and attracts the biggest bookings in the account ($7,472 average). Meanwhile $11 a day runs on two campaigns that produced zero recorded bookings in 90 days, and a generic "luxury" theme burned $44,764 in excess spend over its lifetime.

Winning back a guest costs $68 with our ads, $872 to $1,454 through an OTA.

Brand advertising delivered 163 direct bookings for $11,161 last quarter. The identical booking through an intermediary costs 13 to 21 times more in commission. Detail in the ledger below.

A typo has been running with roughly $10,500 of media behind it.

"Nestled in a magnificiant ancient jungle" is live in six ads, including the two biggest spenders, and Google's own quality grader rates one of them "Excellent." A second typo, "Family-firendly," sits in the asset pool. Both are same-day fixes for a Leading Hotels of the World brand.

Springs and Gardens advertise in accounts nobody on this side can see.

Their websites credit $408,000 and $87,000 of bookings to paid search run from unshared ad accounts, and Gardens' converts at a third of Tented's rate. The cheapest growth move in the portfolio may simply be auditing spend that already exists.

The Toll Economics

Why we advertise on our own name, and why it's the cheap option

When someone searches a Nayara property, the results page stacks paid OTA listings, Google's hotel rate module, and TripAdvisor above our own listing, which sits sixth to eighth. The usual objection to brand advertising, that it pays for clicks the free listing would catch anyway, assumes the free listing ranks first. Ours does not, so pausing brand ads would hand those clicks to Booking.com's ad, not to us.

Google is less our engine than our toll collector, and the escape route is the owned channels in the plan below.

The Gardens Question

Why does Gardens capture only 0.13% of its own name?

Gardens has the largest brand-search demand of the three properties and the worst capture, and it is not one problem but four stacked on top of each other.

1. The rebrand cut the name off from its history. Gardens was Arenal Nayara, the original property, with a 2015 Travel + Leisure No. 1 and a 2016 Condé Nast No. 2 to its name. Those wins, the press, and years of links still live under the old identity, disconnected from "Nayara Gardens" in both Google's index and the AI systems trained on it. The measurable result: Gardens' share of the portfolio's organic traffic fell from 24% to under 15% across 2025, and roughly 1,000 searches a quarter still use the legacy name, plus a steady stream of misspellings ("niara gardens" alone: 303 searches) that nothing currently catches.

2. The brand is fighting itself for Google's trust. The legacy nayaragardens.com site is still live alongside the corporate site, so Google sees two Nayara domains competing for the same name and ranks neither decisively, while each OTA consolidates one strong page. That is how our average position lands at 6.9 for our own property, below the fold of attention.

3. Everything above position 6 is a toll booth. Hotel rate ads, OTA text ads, and TripAdvisor occupy the entire first screen. At position 7, a 0.13% click rate is not an audience rejecting Gardens; it is an audience that never scrolls far enough to see it.

4. Even the paid defense leaks. Gardens does run brand ads, from the account we cannot see, and they convert at 0.20% against Tented's 0.66%. Combined with the broken booking-flow tracking, even the clicks Gardens wins partially vanish from the record.

The encouraging part: every layer of this is fixable without buying new demand. A rebrand bridge page ("Nayara Gardens, formerly Arenal Nayara") with award markup, consolidating or redirecting the legacy domain, repairing the tracking, and auditing the existing ads attacks the cheapest traffic Gardens will ever win, sized directionally at ~$155,000 a quarter in direct-booking recapture at Gardens' own average booking value.

Landing Pages

Build themed pages, test them honestly

The clearest proof that pages, not ads, are the constraint: searches like "nayara tented camp family tent" click through at over 30% and convert at almost zero, because the page they land on never answers the kids question. Every campaign theme deserves a page that keeps the ad's promise: a wellness ad should land on hot springs and the open-air spa, not the generic property page.

Where they live: on the main domain (nayararesorts.com/tented-camp/wellness and similar), never on separate microsites. Same-domain pages keep the booking attribution intact, inherit the domain's trust with Google, and double as the organic and AI-answer page for their topic. One page, three jobs. Brand traffic is exempt: people searching "nayara official website" want the real site and get it.

Page anatomy: a hero image or 15-second loop matching the ad, the ad's promise restated in the headline, a proof strip (Travel + Leisure No. 1, Leading Hotels of the World), three theme modules, one persistent book-direct button that carries tracking cleanly into the booking engine, an FAQ block answering the literal questions people search (all-inclusive, adults-only) marked up for AI citation, and a small "not sure which Nayara?" router so a mismatched guest moves to the right property instead of leaving.

The testing math, stated plainly

About 0.85% of themed-campaign visitors book and about 3% enter the booking engine. A test judged on completed bookings would need roughly 59,000 visitors to detect a 25% improvement, which is over a year of traffic: unusable. Judged on booking-engine entries, the same confidence needs about 16,000 visitors (or 8,300 for a 35% improvement), which the themed campaigns deliver in one to two quarters. So every test runs on booking-engine entry rate as the decision metric, with revenue tracked alongside as the sanity check.

Mechanics: for search campaigns, Google's built-in experiment tool splits traffic 50/50 between the new page and the current one. PMax campaigns cannot split traffic, so there the new page is assigned directly and read before-versus-after in analytics. Test order: first the themed page against the current property page (the big swing), then hero video versus still image, then the booking button's wording. One variable at a time. One exception: no testing on Gardens until its checkout tracking is repaired, because its tests would be unreadable.

The Organic Companion

Making Gardens the answer for "best family resort in Costa Rica," and making AI recommend all three

Gardens: the family flagship, built in five pieces

Family demand is a web-search behavior (it is entirely absent from Maps discovery data), which means content and search are the battlefield, and Gardens has the raw material: family suites, the dining campus that already attracts 3,500+ Maps discoveries a quarter on "restaurants," a 4.9 rating across 1,065 reviews, and the original property's heritage. The build: (1) the rebrand bridge with award markup, reconnecting the buried Travel + Leisure and Condé Nast wins to the new name; (2) a family hub on the corporate domain answering what families actually ask, rooms that sleep four, kids' programming, the sloth trail, getting there with children; (3) the comparison page routing families explicitly to Gardens while sending couples to Springs and adventurers to Tented, which also answers the 400+ quarterly "which Nayara" searches and is the exact page every travel advisor and AI assistant needs; (4) the all-inclusive answer page, converting the ~780 quarterly "all inclusive la fortuna" searchers who find Gardens and currently get no answer; and (5) reviews surfaced as structured evidence in page markup and fact sheets, because "best family resort" claims need third-party proof, and Gardens has 1,065 pieces of it. The paid family campaign at Gardens then launches post-audit onto pages that already make the case, inheriting the copy and learnings from Tented's family-glamping test.

AI visibility: the citation layer for Costa Rica

AI assistants already send Nayara guests: ChatGPT and Gemini referrals converted $21.5K at Tented from a few hundred visits, Gardens leads the new AI traffic channel in our analytics, and Google's search report shows machine-generated evaluation queries testing Nayara against specific categories ("family-friendly luxury costa rica," "best glamping experiences costa rica," "romantic resorts costa rica with hot springs"). Those queries are the target list, and the clock is real: Nekajui, the new Ritz-Carlton Reserve, is filling the AI training corpora in the categories where Nayara is silent.

The program, for all three Costa Rica properties: a structured fact sheet per property that machines can quote cleanly (seasons, distances from SJO, room counts, child policies, awards with years); FAQ markup on every page, answering the literal questions in the search data ("is nayara springs all inclusive," "is nayara springs adults only"); direct-answer opening paragraphs on editorial pieces; comparison content, because assistants reach for "versus" framing when recommending; clean crawlability with an llms.txt on the corporate domain; and third-party corroboration, since AI answers weight reviews, the Leading Hotels listing, and press above a brand's own copy. Two guardrails from the entity work: each property owns its superlative lane so our content never competes with itself (Springs takes "best resort in Costa Rica" claims, Tented takes the Central America titles it holds, Gardens takes "best family resort in Costa Rica"), and Springs gets explicit disambiguation content against The Springs Resort & Spa, which AI systems conflate today. AI referrals become a tracked KPI from day one, so the baseline exists before the curve.

Methodology & caveats

All findings derive from the Windsor.ai data integration spanning the Google Ads account (Tented Camp), GA4 website analytics for all four properties, Google Search Console for nayararesorts.com, and the connected Google Business Profiles, supplemented by all-time ad-account exports. Paid performance is validated against website-recorded bookings; where the ad platform and the website disagree, the website governs. Google Ads access covered Tented Camp only; Springs and Gardens paid recommendations are gated on account sharing and audit. Recapture sizings are directional, computed at each property's own average booking value. OTA-direct bookings are outside this dataset and require the PMS source-of-business report. Award and amenity claims referenced in copy should receive a verification pass before publication.
