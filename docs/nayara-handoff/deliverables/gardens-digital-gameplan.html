1.The opportunity

Gardens is not a failing property. It is a strong property that the machines describe wrongly. The demand and the trust already exist; the job is exposure.

  • It already has world-class trust: 4.9 stars on 1,070 reviews, the most-reviewed of the three properties, plus real rainforest experience. Business Profile

The opportunity in one line: convert the trust Gardens already has into the visibility it does not. That is a far easier job than building demand from nothing, and it is the highest-leverage work in the portfolio.

2.Why we are here (briefly, and we all own it)

Two honest reasons, neither a failure: the property was renamed, so its history, reviews, and awards are not yet fully connected to "Nayara Gardens," and the family story was never exposed in the page structure, the markup, or the content the engines read. The result is that Google and the AI assistants default to describing Gardens as romance. This is a shared responsibility across the team, and it is fixable. The rest of this document is the fix, and a good deal of it is already underway.

Where the sloths fit

The sloth origin and brand story belong to Nayara Tented Camp, where it began, and the sloths are wild, natural rainforest animals, not a captive sanctuary or petting zoo. That is the stronger, more luxury, more sustainable story (we restored the rainforest and the wild sloths stayed). Because the sloths are wild and roam the shared Arenal forest, Gardens uses them cleverly for the family angle: "wild sloths your kids can spot from the trails," a nature hook, never a claim to the origin story. Tented leads the sloth content and AI answer; Gardens borrows the wild-sloth wonder for families.

What Gardens does not chase: hot springs (that is Tabacon, and Gardens has no thermal springs), romance and honeymoon (that is Springs), and any value or entry-level framing. Ceding these cleanly is what keeps the family position sharp.

5.Capturing all-inclusive demand, honestly

"Costa rica all inclusive" is a 49,500-a-month search we cannot rank for as an all-inclusive resort, because we are not one. Here is how we win it anyway, without arguing with the searcher.

The word "all-inclusive" is shorthand for four needs: predictable cost, no nickel-and-diming, food sorted, and activities included. We meet the need without the label, and we have an honest "yes" to offer:

  • The brand-level truth: Costa Rica is breakfast plus experiences, but Bocas del Toro, Alto Atacama, and Hangaroa are genuinely all-inclusive. So "does Nayara have all-inclusive resorts?" is honestly yes, and we route the searcher accordingly.
  • The honest answer page (AEO): lead with the plain answer ("Nayara Gardens is not all-inclusive; here is exactly what is included: daily breakfast, the rainforest and wildlife experiences, kids' programming"), then the value. FAQPage schema. Wins "is nayara gardens all inclusive," which already ranks, plus the La Fortuna all-inclusive searches already hitting us in Maps.
  • The honest guide (corporate): a "Costa Rica all-inclusive resorts: an honest guide" page that fairly explains the landscape, positions Gardens as the family pick with transparent inclusions, and routes true all-inclusive seekers to Bocas and Atacama. This intercepts the big research volume without a false claim.
  • Never lecture. Do not open with "unpackaged is better." Answer the question, meet the need, earn the upgrade.
Why it works: it is honest (which AEO and GEO reward), it monetizes a category we cannot enter as a resort by being the trusted guide, and it uses our real all-inclusive properties as the genuine yes.

8.Why this is a strong strategy

  • It converts strength we already have. The trust, the reviews, the awards, and the AI traffic exist; we are exposing them, which is far cheaper and faster than building demand.
  • It targets winnable ground. Family, rainforest, and dining in Arenal are white space no competitor owns, not the unwinnable head terms.
  • One foundation lifts everything. The EEAT schema work pays into SEO, AEO, GEO, and trust at the same time.
  • It is coordinated and already moving, with clear owners on the site and on the organic side, not a wish list.
  • It is honest and on-brand, the wild-sloth and all-inclusive answers are true, which is exactly what the AI engines reward, and it fits the regenerative story.
  • It is measurable, against a real baseline and an AI-answer scoreboard, to Alex's provability standard.
