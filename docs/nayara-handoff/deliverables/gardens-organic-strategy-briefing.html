Digital Marketing Briefing

Nayara Gardens: Organic Search Strategy

Where we stand, what we have decided, and the plan from here.

Prepared June 2026  ·  Digital Marketing

The short version

Gardens is our single biggest opportunity in search, and also our most urgent fix. It draws more search interest than either sister property, yet it turns very little of that interest into bookings. At the same time, Google and the AI assistants describe it as a couples retreat, when it is in fact our family flagship. The plan puts focused effort on Gardens now, leans on content and cleanup rather than new ad spend, and leaves Springs and Tented Camp on normal maintenance because they are healthy. Some of the cleanup is already underway and the most important piece has already gone through.

What is holding Gardens back

  1. It is still tied to its old name. The resort was renamed, but across Google, the AI tools, and many travel sites it still appears under older names such as "Arenal Nayara." The reviews, history, and major awards it earned under those names are not fully connected to "Nayara Gardens," so years of earned credit are not flowing to the current name.
  2. It is being described as a romance resort. Both Google and the AI assistants present Gardens as a couples retreat, even though it is our family property, with suites that sleep four, the sloth trail, and children's programming. This is an active error, not just a gap, and it quietly steers families toward someone else.
  3. Large search interest, very few bookings from it. Gardens draws a high volume of searches, more than Springs or Tented, but captures little of it. The demand is clearly there. The site is not converting it, partly because it sits below the booking sites on its own name, and partly because the family story is missing from the pages.

Why Gardens first, and not Springs or Tented

Springs and Tented Camp are in good shape. Their positioning is clear, they convert well, and their search presence is healthy, so they do not need the same all-hands attention right now. Gardens is the one property that is both the weakest performer and the most misunderstood, which is why it earns the concentrated effort. Springs and Tented continue on normal maintenance, and we return to them once Gardens is on track. This is a sequencing decision, not neglect.

What we have decided, and why

Keep the work on the Gardens website for now. Moving Gardens content onto the corporate site while the Gardens site is still live would put two of our own sites in competition for the same name and weaken both. We consolidate later, carefully, as a planned step rather than a rushed one.

Fix the name, the family positioning, and the awards mostly behind the scenes. Most of this is done through the data that search engines and AI read, and by correcting how partner and travel sites list the property. It does not require a public "we used to be called something else" page, which keeps the brand presentation clean.

Build a small set of family-focused pages. Each one is designed to do double duty: the page Google ranks for family searches, and the page the AI assistants quote when someone asks about family travel in the area.

Lead with content and cleanup, not new ad spend. The gap here is visibility and positioning, so the highest-return work is correcting the record and building the right pages.

Already underway

The reconnection work has started, and the highest-impact item is complete.

  • Done: the name correction on our Google Maps profile, the single most important listing of all, has already gone through.
  • Requested: name corrections on major travel directories that still show the old name, including arenal.net and visitcostarica.com.
  • Requested: corrections to the Condé Nast award listing and booking record, so the awards attach to the current name.

The plan from here

  1. Turn on full search measurement for the Gardens site. Its search data is not currently reaching us, and we need it to track progress.
  2. Finish reconnecting the name, history, and awards across the remaining listings and through the data that search and AI read.
  3. Build the family pages. This is the core fix for the couples misperception.
  4. Add answer pages for the questions guests actually ask, such as dining and what is included in a stay.
  5. Add a page for the resident sloths, a genuine point of difference no competitor can copy.
  6. Make sure every page feeds the AI assistants cleanly, since Gardens already leads our AI-driven bookings.

What success looks like

Gardens described correctly as a family resort across both search and the AI assistants, its awards reconnected to the current name, a larger share of its considerable search interest turning into direct bookings, and continued growth in AI-assistant bookings. The work is mostly content and cleanup, low in cost, and measurable once tracking is fully in place.
