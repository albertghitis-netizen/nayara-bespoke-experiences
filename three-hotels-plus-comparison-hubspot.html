The Idea

One interconnected destination

The intimacy of your own secluded hotel, with the experiences of three world-class properties within reach.

At Nayara Gardens, Nayara Springs, and Nayara Tented Camp, each property keeps its own character and quiet, yet guests share one ecosystem of wellness and adventure that turns a stay into a journey through a single magical rainforest. Whether you want rejuvenation or nature immersion, everything is within reach. You are not choosing between three hotels. You are choosing one interconnected destination.

Which leaves one happy question: where do you make your home base. First, what every guest shares.

Wellness Through Movement

Yoga in the rainforest

Vinyasa Yoga keeps the body flowing and energized, linking breath to movement. The practice takes on another dimension when your mat is surrounded by howler monkeys and tropical birds, and the forest becomes part of the flow.

Mindfulness Yoga invites you to slow down and find stillness amid the symphony of the rainforest, less about exertion and more about presence, for people who have forgotten what it feels like to simply be. Both are offered across the properties, so you can practice at the edge of a volcanic valley, beside a hot spring, or above the canopy.

Costa Rica has over 900 bird species, many visible from the reserve.

Nature Immersion

Three ways to explore

The Botanical Nature Hike unveils the plant life that sustains the ecosystem, from towering ceiba trees to medicinal species used for centuries. Your guide does not just point at trees; they tell the story of a forest that has been healing itself, and others, for millennia.

The Bird Watching experience connects you to the avian diversity that makes Costa Rica a paradise for birders. With more than 900 species in the country and dozens visible from the reserve, every morning walk becomes a masterclass in color, sound, and flight.

And Finding Tony the Sloth is exactly what it sounds like: a gentle, guided search for a wild two-toed sloth who has made the reserve his home, a living symbol of why protecting the forest is a responsibility, not a marketing strategy.

Las Thermas

Where earth meets wellness

Las Thermas at Nayara Tented Camp offers something rare: natural hot springs heated by geothermal energy deep beneath the rainforest floor. More than a spa amenity, it is a place to soak in warmth, watch the night sky, and feel the ancient power of the earth beneath you.

The springs are fed by the same volcanic system that powers Arenal, water that has traveled through layers of rock, gathering minerals along the way. The result is a bathing experience that is not manufactured or chlorinated, but genuinely geological. You are soaking in the earth's own warmth, surrounded by forest, under stars.

Which Nayara Is Right For You

Three resorts, three characters

Everything above is shared. What is not shared is the feeling of each place. The three resorts sit on one reserve, but they are not three of the same thing. Most guests pick a home base that fits the trip, then roam the rest.

Nayara Gardens The family flagship

The original Nayara, set among lush gardens with wild sloths in the trees. Suites that sleep four, kids' programming, and the most-reviewed of the three resorts at 4.9 across more than 1,070 reviews.

Best for: families and multigenerational groups.

Nayara Springs Adults-only romance

Adults-only and serene, built for couples. Private villas with their own hot-spring plunge pools, and Costa Rica's only Three-Key Michelin dining, in the Relais & Chateaux tradition.

Best for: couples, honeymooners, and a quiet wellness reset.

Nayara Tented Camp Adventure and seclusion

Clifftop canvas suites with volcano views and the Las Thermas hot springs at the door. Named the number one resort in Costa Rica by Travel + Leisure readers.

Best for: adventure seekers, and couples or families with older children who want the wow-factor room.

Your home base sets the tone. The shared reserve gives you all of it.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can guests use activities across all three properties?
Yes. Guests at any of the three connected Costa Rica resorts have seamless access to the wellness, nature, and adventure experiences across all of them.
Which Nayara resort is best for families?
Nayara Gardens, the family flagship. It has suites that sleep four, kids' programming, wild sloths on the trails, and it is the most-reviewed of the three resorts. From there, families can still enjoy the experiences across all three.
Which Nayara resort is best for couples?
Nayara Springs, which is adults-only, with private plunge-pool villas and Michelin-recognized dining. Couples who want adventure also love Nayara Tented Camp for its clifftop canvas suites and volcano views.
What yoga is offered?
Two practices, Vinyasa and Mindfulness, offered across the properties in settings from a volcanic valley to a hot spring or a canopy platform.
What guided nature experiences are there?
A botanical nature hike, birdwatching (Costa Rica has over 900 bird species), and Finding Tony the Sloth, a guided search for a wild two-toed sloth living in the reserve.
What is Las Thermas?
Natural hot springs at Nayara Tented Camp, heated by the same geothermal system that powers Arenal Volcano and rich in minerals, set beneath the rainforest canopy.
