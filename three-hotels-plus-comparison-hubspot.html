Best of Both Worlds

Three hotels with their own soul, one rainforest they share

At the foot of the Arenal Volcano sit three small resorts. Each has its own character, its own quiet, and its own front door. And each shares a single living rainforest reserve.

That is the rare thing here, and it is worth saying plainly: you get the intimacy of a boutique hotel that is unmistakably itself, with the run of an estate far larger than any one of them. You book one identity. You inherit all three.

Most travelers agonize over the choice between a family resort, a romantic hideaway, and an adventure lodge. At Nayara in Costa Rica, you do not have to choose between them. You choose where you sleep. Everything else is shared.

So the only real decision is which one becomes your home base.

Which Nayara Is Right For You

Three resorts, three characters

The original Nayara and the family flagship, set among lush gardens with wild sloths in the trees. Suites that sleep four, kids' programming, and the most-reviewed of the three, rated 4.9 across more than 1,000 guest reviews.

Best for: families and multigenerational groups.

Best for: adventure seekers, and couples or families with older children who want the wow-factor room.

Adults-only and serene, built for two. Private villas with their own hot-spring plunge pools, and Costa Rica's only Three-Key Michelin hotel, in the Relais & Chateaux tradition.

Best for: couples, honeymooners, and a quiet wellness reset.

Choose one. Inherit all three.

The Shared Reserve

What every guest gets, whichever door they choose

Pick your home base for its character, then let the rest of the reserve open up. Five restaurants, the Spa Arenal, the guided rainforest trails, the yoga decks, and the hot springs are open to you no matter which resort you book. Private as a boutique, shared as an estate. That is the whole idea.

Wellness Through Movement

Yoga in the rainforest

Vinyasa Yoga keeps the body flowing and energized, breath linked to movement. Practiced here, with howler monkeys calling overhead and tropical birds crossing the clearing, the forest stops being scenery and becomes part of the flow.

Mindfulness Yoga asks for the opposite: stillness, held inside the rainforest's own slow symphony. Less about exertion, more about presence, for anyone who has forgotten what it feels like simply to be. Both practices are offered across the properties, so you might unroll a mat at the edge of a volcanic valley one morning, beside a hot spring the next, and above the canopy the morning after.

Costa Rica has over 900 bird species, many visible from the reserve.

Nature Immersion

Three guided ways to read the forest

The Botanical Nature Hike unveils the plant life that sustains the ecosystem, from towering ceiba trees to medicinal species used for centuries. Your guide does not simply point at trees. They tell the story of a forest that has been healing itself, and others, for millennia.

The Bird Watching experience opens up the avian diversity that makes Costa Rica a paradise for birders. With more than 900 species in the country and dozens visible from the reserve, an early walk becomes a masterclass in color, sound, and flight.

And Finding Tony the Sloth is exactly what it sounds like: a gentle, guided search for a wild two-toed sloth who has made the reserve his home. He is a living reminder of why protecting this forest is a responsibility, not a marketing line.

Las Thermas

Where the earth does the heating

Las Thermas at Nayara Tented Camp offers something rare: natural hot springs warmed by geothermal energy deep beneath the rainforest floor. It is less a spa amenity than a place to lower yourself into the warmth, watch the night sky open, and feel the age of the ground under you.

The springs are fed by the same volcanic system that powers Arenal, water that has traveled through layers of rock and gathered minerals along the way. The result is a bath that is not manufactured or chlorinated but genuinely geological. You are soaking in the earth's own warmth, surrounded by forest, under the stars.

The Nayara Difference

A day that flows like the forest

A morning might begin with Vinyasa at Nayara Springs, then a botanical hike through the Tented Camp reserve, an afternoon lowered into Las Thermas, and a sunset Mindfulness session as the volcano turns to silhouette. No transfers. No logistics. No friction. Just a day that moves as naturally as the forest around it.

Every hike, every class, every soak points back to one idea: that a great stay feeds the mind and the senses as much as the body. That is the rare thing the three Nayara resorts offer together, the chance to meet Costa Rica at its own pace, with a different character to come home to each night and the same rainforest holding all of it.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which Nayara resort is best for families?
Nayara Gardens, the family flagship: suites that sleep four, kids' programming, wild sloths on the trails, and the most-reviewed of the three resorts. From there, families can still enjoy the experiences across all three.
Which Nayara resort is best for couples?
Nayara Springs, which is adults-only, with private plunge-pool villas and Three-Key Michelin recognition. Couples who want adventure also love Nayara Tented Camp for its clifftop canvas suites and volcano views.
What makes Nayara in Costa Rica different?
Three connected resorts share one rainforest reserve, so you book the resort whose character fits your trip, then roam the wellness, nature, dining, and adventure of all three on foot.
Can guests use activities across all three properties?
Yes. Guests at any of the three connected resorts have seamless access to the wellness, nature, and adventure experiences, the five restaurants, and the Spa Arenal across all of them.
What yoga is offered?
Two practices, Vinyasa and Mindfulness, offered across the properties in settings from a volcanic valley to a hot spring or a canopy platform.
What is Las Thermas?
Natural hot springs at Nayara Tented Camp, heated by the same geothermal system that powers Arenal Volcano and rich in minerals, set beneath the rainforest canopy.
