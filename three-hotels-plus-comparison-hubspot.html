The Idea

One rainforest, three ways to call it home

At the foot of Arenal Volcano, three resorts share a single living reserve. You book one room. What you actually inherit is the whole forest, and three places to roam it from.

Nayara Gardens, Nayara Springs, and Nayara Tented Camp each keep their own character and their own quiet. Yet a guest at any one of them can walk freely into the wellness, nature, and adventure of all three. The romance of one, the seclusion of another, the family ease of the third, threaded together by the same canopy and the same volcanic spring water beneath it. Five restaurants, the Spa Arenal, the guided rainforest trails, the yoga decks: all of it is open to you, whichever resort you book.

It means you choose the room that fits the trip without giving anything up. You are not deciding between three hotels. You are choosing one destination that happens to offer three ways to live inside it.

Which leaves only one real question: where do you make your home base? Before that, here is what every guest shares, whichever door they walk through.

You book a room. You inherit a reserve.

Wellness Through Movement

Yoga in the rainforest

Vinyasa Yoga keeps the body flowing and energized, breath linked to movement. Practiced here, with howler monkeys calling overhead and tropical birds crossing the clearing, the forest stops being scenery and becomes part of the flow.

Mindfulness Yoga asks for the opposite: stillness, held inside the rainforest's own slow symphony. Less about exertion, more about presence, for anyone who has forgotten what it feels like simply to be. Both practices are offered across the properties, so you might unroll a mat at the edge of a volcanic valley one morning, beside a hot spring the next, and above the canopy the morning after.

Costa Rica has over 900 bird species, many visible from the reserve.

Nature Immersion

Three guided ways to read the forest

The Botanical Nature Hike unveils the plant life that sustains the ecosystem, from towering ceiba trees to medicinal species used for centuries. Your guide does not simply point at trees. They tell the story of a forest that has been healing itself, and others, for millennia.

The Bird Watching experience opens up the avian diversity that makes Costa Rica a paradise for birders. With more than 900 species in the country and dozens visible from the reserve, an early walk becomes a masterclass in color, sound, and flight.

And Finding Tony the Sloth is exactly what it sounds like: a gentle, guided search for a wild two-toed sloth who has made the reserve his home. He is a living reminder of why protecting this forest is a responsibility, not a marketing line.

Las Thermas

Where the earth does the heating

Las Thermas at Nayara Tented Camp offers something rare: natural hot springs warmed by geothermal energy deep beneath the rainforest floor. It is less a spa amenity than a place to lower yourself into the warmth, watch the night sky open, and feel the age of the ground under you.

The springs are fed by the same volcanic system that powers Arenal, water that has traveled through layers of rock and gathered minerals along the way. The result is a bath that is not manufactured or chlorinated but genuinely geological. You are soaking in the earth's own warmth, surrounded by forest, under the stars.

Which Nayara Is Right For You

Three resorts, three characters

Everything above is shared. What is not shared is the feeling of each place. The three resorts sit on one reserve, but they are not three versions of the same thing. Most guests pick the home base that fits the trip, then roam the rest.

Nayara Gardens The family flagship

The original Nayara, set among lush gardens with wild sloths in the trees. Suites that sleep four, kids' programming, and the most-reviewed of the three resorts, rated 4.9 across more than 1,000 guest reviews.

Best for: families and multigenerational groups.

Nayara Springs Adults-only romance

Adults-only and serene, built for couples. Private villas with their own hot-spring plunge pools, and Costa Rica's only Three-Key Michelin hotel, in the Relais & Chateaux tradition.

Best for: couples, honeymooners, and a quiet wellness reset.

Nayara Tented Camp Adventure and seclusion

Clifftop canvas suites with volcano views and the Las Thermas hot springs at the door. Named the number one resort in Central America by Travel + Leisure readers.

Best for: adventure seekers, and couples or families with older children who want the wow-factor room.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can guests use activities across all three properties?
Yes. Guests at any of the three connected Costa Rica resorts have seamless access to the wellness, nature, and adventure experiences across all of them.
Which Nayara resort is best for families?
Nayara Gardens, the family flagship. It has suites that sleep four, kids' programming, wild sloths on the trails, and it is the most-reviewed of the three resorts. From there, families can still enjoy the experiences across all three.
Which Nayara resort is best for couples?
Nayara Springs, which is adults-only, with private plunge-pool villas and Michelin-recognized dining. Couples who want adventure also love Nayara Tented Camp for its clifftop canvas suites and volcano views.
What yoga is offered?
Two practices, Vinyasa and Mindfulness, offered across the properties in settings from a volcanic valley to a hot spring or a canopy platform.
What guided nature experiences are there?
A botanical nature hike, birdwatching (Costa Rica has over 900 bird species), and Finding Tony the Sloth, a guided search for a wild two-toed sloth living in the reserve.
What is Las Thermas?
Natural hot springs at Nayara Tented Camp, heated by the same geothermal system that powers Arenal Volcano and rich in minerals, set beneath the rainforest canopy.
