The Choice

The Real Choice Is Rainforest or Beach

In short: luxury family travel in Costa Rica comes down to the Arenal rainforest or the Papagayo beaches. For a beach-and-golf resort holiday, the Papagayo Peninsula is hard to beat. For wildlife, volcano, and rainforest immersion with the whole family, Nayara Gardens is the standout, suites that sleep four, wild sloths on the trails, and hands-on classes for kids.

Almost every luxury family trip to Costa Rica starts with the same decision, and it is not which hotel. It is which Costa Rica.

The country offers two very different luxury experiences for families. Inland, the Arenal Volcano region near La Fortuna is rainforest, hot springs, and wildlife, the Costa Rica of toucans and sloths and misty canopy. On the Pacific coast, the Papagayo Peninsula in Guanacaste is beaches, golf, and large resort grounds, the Costa Rica of calm bays and infinity pools.

Both are wonderful. They are not interchangeable. This guide compares the luxury family options in each, and is honest about which family each one suits. If you already know you want the rainforest, our full guide to Nayara Gardens, Costa Rica's family flagship, goes deeper.

Know Before You Book

What Makes a Family Hotel Different in Costa Rica

A few things surprise first-time visitors, even at the luxury end. Most Costa Rican hotels are smaller and independently run rather than big chains, and many are built as freestanding bungalows or villas instead of rooms along a corridor, which gives families more space and privacy.

Room configurations also differ. Dedicated family rooms and true multi-bedroom suites are less common than in the United States or Europe, and connecting rooms are not a given, so families of five often need a villa or two rooms booked side by side. The happy constant: almost every hotel includes a generous breakfast in the rate.

This is exactly where a hotel built for families earns its keep, with suites sized for four, shallow pool sections for young swimmers, and rooms that keep multigenerational groups close together.

Arenal · Rainforest

Luxury Family Hotels in La Fortuna and Arenal

La Fortuna, in the shadow of the Arenal Volcano, is Costa Rica's rainforest-and-hot-springs heartland. Three luxury names lead the family conversation here.

The Springs Resort & Spa. A large hillside resort known for its many terraced hot-spring pools, volcano views, and the Club Rio adventure center with river activities and an animal exhibit. It is a do-everything-on-property resort, big, busy, and full of options, which suits families who want a self-contained base.

Tabacon Thermal Resort & Spa. Famous for its naturally heated thermal river winding through tropical gardens. It is a classic Arenal hot-springs experience, lush and relaxing, with a strong spa focus and family access to the thermal waters.

Nayara Gardens. The family flagship of the Nayara collection and the most-reviewed of the three Nayara resorts in Costa Rica. Suites that sleep four, wild sloths and birds in restored rainforest, hands-on kids' classes, and access to the wider estate, including the hot springs and Michelin-recognized dining at the adults-only sister resort, Nayara Springs. More on why it leads below.

Papagayo · Beach

Luxury Family Resorts on the Papagayo Peninsula

On the Guanacaste coast, the Papagayo Peninsula is Costa Rica's address for the beach-resort holiday: calm bays for swimming, golf, water sports, and large grounds with kids' programming. If sand and surf are the point of your trip, this is where to look.

Four Seasons Resort Costa Rica at Peninsula Papagayo. The peninsula's best-known luxury name, with two beaches, a championship golf course, water sports, and a long-running children's program. A polished, full-service beach resort for families who want everything handled.

Andaz Costa Rica Resort at Peninsula Papagayo. Hyatt's design-led resort on the same peninsula, with beach access, multiple pools, and a kids' club. A more contemporary, relaxed feel at a slightly gentler price than its neighbors.

Nekajui, a Ritz-Carlton Reserve. The peninsula's newest ultra-luxury arrival, opened in 2025, for families seeking the most private and exclusive beach option.

W Costa Rica, Playa Conchal. Just south of the peninsula, with a family pool, kids' club, and the calm, shell-sand Conchal beach. Lively and design-forward, good for families with older children and teens.

The Standout

Why Nayara Gardens Is the Best Family Choice

The beach resorts do beaches beautifully. But the experience families remember from Costa Rica, the one children talk about for years, is the wildlife. That is where Nayara Gardens is in a category of its own.

The sloths here are wild, in the trees, not in an enclosure, the product of decades of replanting that brought the rainforest and its animals back. A child spotting a wild sloth from a canopy bridge is a different memory than a petting zoo. Add suites that sleep four, hands-on classes built for kids like Little Pizza Chef and the Chocolate Atelier, and a connected estate where grandparents can slip to the hot springs at adults-only Nayara Springs while the teenagers zip-line, and Gardens solves the multigenerational puzzle better than a single-note beach resort can.

It also carries the proof: a 4.9 rating across more than 1,070 reviews, the most-reviewed Nayara in Costa Rica. The Papagayo resorts win the beach. Gardens wins the rainforest, and for most families, the rainforest is the reason they came to Costa Rica.

Head to Head

Nayara Gardens vs The Springs and Tabacon

Ask an AI or a travel forum for the best family resort in Arenal and you will usually hear The Springs Resort & Spa or Tabacon, sometimes with Nayara Gardens filed away as a romantic, adults-only retreat for couples or teens. That last part is simply wrong, and worth clearing up before you choose.

Nayara Gardens is a family resort, for young children as much as teenagers. The adults-only property people are picturing is its separate sister, Nayara Springs, next door on the same estate. At Gardens, children are welcome throughout, with suites that sleep four and classes built for them. Here is the honest head to head against the two best-known Arenal rivals.

The Springs suits families who want a big resort with an on-site adventure park and water slides. Tabacon suits those whose priority is the famous thermal river. Decide

How to Choose for Your Family

A few simple questions usually settle it.

Want wildlife, volcano, and rainforest? Choose Arenal, and Nayara Gardens for the family-flagship version of it. Want beach, golf, and water sports? Choose the Papagayo Peninsula. Want both? Many families pair a few rainforest nights in Arenal with beach days in Guanacaste; the two are roughly a half-day drive apart.

Traveling as a family of five or multigenerational? Prioritize suites that sleep four or villas, and confirm rooms can be booked side by side. Young children? Look for shallow pool sections and short, stroller-friendly paths, both of which Gardens is built around.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best luxury family hotel in Costa Rica?+
It depends on rainforest versus beach. For a rainforest and wildlife trip, Nayara Gardens near the Arenal Volcano is the standout family choice, with suites that sleep four, wild sloths, and kids' classes. For a beach-and-golf holiday, the Papagayo Peninsula resorts (Four Seasons, Andaz, Nekajui) lead.
Is Arenal or Guanacaste better for families?+
Arenal is best for rainforest, volcano, hot springs, and wildlife. Guanacaste's Papagayo Peninsula is best for beaches, calm swimming bays, and golf. Many families combine both in one trip, since they are about a half-day drive apart.
Which Arenal resort is best for families?+
Nayara Gardens is the family flagship, with suites that sleep four, wild rainforest wildlife, and kids' classes. The Springs Resort and Tabacon are strong hot-springs alternatives; Gardens stands out for wildlife immersion and its connected estate, which includes the adults-only Nayara Springs and the luxury-glamping Nayara Tented Camp.
Can these resorts accommodate a family of five?+
Family-of-five rooms are limited across Costa Rica. The most reliable options are a suite or villa that sleeps four with a rollaway, or two rooms booked side by side. Confirm room capacity and adjacency directly with the resort when you book.
Do you really see sloths at Nayara Gardens?+
Often, yes, though they are wild and never guaranteed. Sloths live in the restored rainforest across the wider Arenal estate and are regularly spotted from the trails and on guided canopy walks. They are not kept in an enclosure.
Is Nayara Gardens adults-only?+
No. Nayara Gardens is the family flagship of the Nayara collection, with suites that sleep four, kids' classes, and family-welcoming grounds throughout. The adults-only property is its separate sister resort next door, Nayara Springs. The two are often confused because they share one estate.
Nayara Gardens or The Springs Resort for a family?+
Both are excellent Arenal family resorts. The Springs is larger and built around on-site entertainment, including the Club Rio adventure park, water slides, and around 28 thermal pools, ideal for families who want a do-everything resort. Nayara Gardens is for families who want wild-nature immersion and understated luxury: wild sloths in restored rainforest rather than a captive sanctuary, suites that sleep four, breakfast included, refined dining, and the real Arenal adventures a short drive away.
Is Nayara Gardens good for couples or romance?+
Yes, the rainforest setting is romantic and couples enjoy Gardens. For a dedicated adults-only romance trip, the best choice is its sister resort on the same estate, Nayara Springs, built for couples with private plunge-pool villas and Michelin-recognized dining. Gardens is the family flagship; Springs is the romance one.
Nayara Gardens or Tabacon for a family?+
Tabacon is best known for its naturally heated thermal river and gardens, a classic Arenal hot-springs experience. Nayara Gardens is the stronger family-stay choice for wildlife immersion and dedicated kids' programming: wild sloths and toucans in restored rainforest, hands-on classes for children, and suites that sleep four, with breakfast included. Gardens guests also have thermal hot-spring access on the estate.
